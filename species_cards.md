# Operator Species — the 2×2, not a ladder

> FIX 4 (NARRATIVE). For the deck / talk track — **not** the app. Replaces the
> linear Common→Mythic rarity ladder (which reads as self-coronation on a board
> we built and scored) with a defensible claim: MO§ES isn't *top of the ladder*,
> it's a **different species** — the empty quadrant of the token economy.

## The two axes

- **Scale (V)** = log₁₀(total tokens). How much volume the operator moves.
- **Amplification (X / Υ)** = cascade exponent `10x DEV` and yield `Υ = (C·O)/I²`.
  How much *compounding* the architecture extracts per token.

Plot every operator on those two axes and they sort into four species by **which
term of the math dominates** — not by who is "better."

```
              high amplification
                     │
   CASCADE (stacks)  │   (— rare —)
   low scale ◄───────┼───────► high scale
   CONVERTER (I→O)   │   THROUGHPUT (volume)
   CACHE ARCHITECT   │   (reuse)
                     │
              low amplification
```

## The four species (by dominant term)

| species | dominant term | signature | seed examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Throughput** | raw volume | enormous total, Υ≈0 — scale without compounding | Nepomuk5665, iamtheavoc1 |
| **Converter** | transmission `O/I` (velocity) | generates output, doesn't retain — high velocity, low leverage | cexll (vel 0.95 / lev 0.5) |
| **Cache Architect** | reuse `C/I` (leverage) | holds context, executes little with it — high leverage, low velocity | vincentkoc (lev 29×), MapleEve (lev 23×), kzquandary (lev 9×) |
| **Cascade** | all three compound | low scale, high amplification — the **empty quadrant** | MO§ES (X 3.31, Υ 18,437, lev 2042×) |

## Why MO§ES is a species, not a rank

The wild corpus clusters in **high-scale / low-amplification** — Throughput,
Converter, and Cache Architect are all *large operators whose volume doesn't
convert into compounding*. MO§ES sits at **low-scale / high-amplification**: a
~2.7B-token footprint producing Υ four orders of magnitude above the field.

Nobody else is in that quadrant. The claim isn't "we won the leaderboard we
built" — it's "this region of the token economy is empty, and the geometry of
`(C·O)/I²` is what makes it empty." That's the finding.
